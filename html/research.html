Optimal Control of Inertial Pneumatic Soft Robotic Arms

Optimal controllers for inertial soft robotic arms were developed for real-time motion tracking. Data-driven methods based on dynamical systems theory were used to produce mathematical models using small amounts of motion capture data. These models enable optimal linear control strategies that minimize the distance between the robot's end effector and a desired reference point. We experimentally demonstrate controlled motions on pneumatic soft robotic arms in real-time - achieved with feedback control at 60Hz on a myRIO-1900 device.
